# 02 - The mechanism: why a Keller map can fail to be injective

Everything on this page is OUR exact result (EXP-002, EXP-004 lemma), verified over $\mathbb{Q}$.

## Weighted skew-product structure

$F$ is equivariant for the $\mathbb{G}_m$-action with source weights $(1, -1, -2)$ and output
weights $(-2, -1, 1)$:
$$F(\lambda x,\ y/\lambda,\ z/\lambda^2) = \left(P/\lambda^2,\ Q/\lambda,\ \lambda R\right).$$
The weight-0 invariants are $v = xy$ and $t = x^2 z$ (with $u = 1 + v$), and
$$a_1 = x^2 P, \qquad b_1 = x Q, \qquad c_1 = R/x = 2 - 3v - t$$
are polynomials in $(v, t)$ alone. Each component is affine in $z$ with coefficient vector
$(u^3, 3xu^2, -x^3)$.

## The reduced Keller identity (two variables, not three)

With output invariants $V' = QR = b_1 c_1$ and $T' = P R^2 = a_1 c_1^2$, the skew-product chain
rule gives, for ANY reduced data (verified with fully symbolic coefficients),
$$\det JF \cdot c_1^2 = -J_2, \qquad J_2 = \det \frac{\partial(V', T')}{\partial(v, t)}.$$
For the announced $F$: $J_2 = 2 c_1^2$, hence $\det JF = -2$. The Keller property is a
TWO-variable identity in the invariant plane; the third dimension only carries the weights.

## The fiber cubic

With $w = u c_1 / 2$ and $\Phi(w) = w^2 - w^3$:
$$\Phi(w) = \frac{w\, QR - P R^2}{4} \quad \text{identically.}$$
Given a target $(A, B, C)$, preimages correspond to roots of the cubic
$4\Phi(w) - w\,BC + AC^2 = 0$: generically three. At the announced target $(-\tfrac14, 0, 0)$
the cubic degenerates to $\Phi(w) = 0$ with roots $\{0, 0, 1\}$, and the three preimages carry
$w$-values $(1, 0, 0)$ exactly (EXP-002).

## Where injectivity dies: non-properness at infinity

A Keller map is everywhere a local diffeomorphism, so two preimage sheets can never merge at a
finite point (that would force $\det JF \to 0$). What fails is PROPERNESS: preimages can escape
to infinity while their images stay bounded, so $F$ is not a covering onto its image and global
injectivity is lost. This is the classical loophole (a proper Keller map IS invertible), the same
one exploited in the real setting by Pinchuk (1994), and the modern face of Kraus's 1884 gap
(ramification at infinity). The exact asymptotic variety of $F$ is queued as JCB-005/EXP-007.

## The generating identity

$a_1 = u b_1/2 - u^2 + u^3 c_1/2$: the first component carries no independent information; the
map is generated by the pair $(b_1, c_1)$. This observation is what the general constructor
([03-seed-family.md](03-seed-family.md)) turns into a machine.
