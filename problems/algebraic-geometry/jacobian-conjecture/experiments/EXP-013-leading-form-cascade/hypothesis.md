# EXP-013 - The leading-form cascade: our rigidity theorem as the local model of JC(2)

- **Question.** For a planar Keller map F = (P, Q), what do its weighted leading forms have to
  look like at EVERY weight ray, and how far does that constrain (or exclude) a counterexample
  at small degrees?
- **Motivation.** JC-P5 route 1 (the tropical sweep). At a ray omega = (w1, w2), w1, w2 >= 1,
  the omega-leading forms (P_om, Q_om) are omega-homogeneous, i.e. exactly the equivariant
  objects of EXP-010. If the top graded piece of det JF has positive omega-degree, constancy
  forces J(P_om, Q_om) = 0: the leading forms are Jacobian-DEPENDENT, and the classical
  structure for that (two omega-homogeneous polynomials with vanishing Jacobian are, up to
  scalars, powers of a common omega-homogeneous form) is the degenerate boundary of our
  equivariant classification. Cascading this constraint along rays is the modern face of the
  Abhyankar/Newton-polygon program; this experiment mechanizes its first floors.
- **Falsifiable predictions.**
  1. [MV] (Dependence at the top ray) For the ordinary degree forms (omega = (1, 1)): on a
     library of REAL Keller maps (compositions of elementary de Joncquieres and affine maps,
     degrees 2..8), whenever deg P + deg Q > 2 the leading forms satisfy J(P_+, Q_+) = 0, and
     each pair factors as P_+ = a h^p, Q_+ = b h^q with a COMMON form h (verified by exact
     factorization); moreover p deg h = deg P, q deg h = deg Q.
  2. [MV] (Ray sweep: the local model everywhere) For the same library and a fan sample of
     rays omega in {(1,1), (2,1), (1,2), (3,1), (1,3), (3,2), (2,3)}: at every ray, either the
     omega-top piece of det JF is already the constant, or J(P_om, Q_om) = 0 with the
     power-of-common-form structure. Every real Keller map is, at every ray, the DEGENERATE
     boundary of the EXP-010 equivariant classification. (Design note, recorded: a first sketch
     of a "two-floor coprime kill" was found insufficient BEFORE running: the second graded
     floor admits solutions, so the classical kill needs the full descent; that deeper cascade
     is queued, not claimed.)
  3. [MV] (Exhaustive small-degree JC(2)) Direct exact solves of the FULL Keller condition for
     generic maps of degree pairs (2,2), (2,3) and, if the solve completes, (3,3): every
     solution branch, instantiated and lifted, has in-image fiber size <= 1. JC(2) verified
     mechanically at these sizes by our own certificate (classical in content, ours in form).
- **Method.** sympy over QQ: automorphism library generated by composing random elementary maps
  (bounded coefficients, seeds fixed); leading forms by graded truncation; dependence and
  common-form structure by exact Jacobian and factor_list; the coprime kill by
  undetermined-coefficient solves of the second graded floor with Rabinowitsch nondegeneracy.
- **Success criterion.** Predictions 1-3 verified in the declared windows.
- **Failure criterion.** A library Keller map violating dependence (would refute the framework,
  i.e. a bug or a misstatement: escalate), or a coprime pair admitting a second-floor solution
  (would be a sensational lead: escalate to a full lift attempt immediately).
